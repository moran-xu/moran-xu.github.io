Welcome to Maoran Xu's webpage! I am a Postoctoral Associate Researcher in Statistics at Duke University. I am working with Drs. Amy Herring and David Dunson on developing flexible probabilistic models for inferring low-dimensional structure in high-dimensional and structured data. Previously, I obtain my doctoral degree in statistics under the supervision of Dr. Leo Duan.

My research is mainly about inference and uncertainty quantification for various types of data and models. For example, high-dimensional data, network data, latent variable models, and cluster models. Specifically, I am interested in using optimization tools in Bayesian methods to build new likelihood-based models on low-dimensional geometric spaces.
